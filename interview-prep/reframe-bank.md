## The Big Three (memorize these)

### "Tell me about yourself" (2-3 min)

> "I'm a full-stack creative strategist focused on direct response and performance creative. I combine copywriting, video production, and AI-generated creative with an analytical mindset from my finance background.
>
> I got here through an unusual path. I spent 6 years in B2B SaaS sales, having thousands of real conversations with buyers. I learned what makes people say yes - the emotional triggers, the objections, the proof they need to commit. I realized the part I was best at was the persuasion itself - the pitch, the copy, the angle that lands.
>
> I took time to handle a family situation, and used that period to build seriously in performance creative. I studied under the DR copywriting tradition - Schwartz, Georgi, Halbert - and built a portfolio of email sequences, advertorials, landing pages, and video scripts. I also picked up AI creative production tools, so I can go from concept to finished ad without a production team.
>
> Now I'm looking for a role where I can apply all of that: the buyer psychology, the creative execution, and the analytical thinking. I want to build and test creative at scale."

### "Why should we hire you?" (60-90 sec)

> "Three things I bring that are hard to find in one person.
>
> First, I understand why people buy. Not theoretically - from 6 years of real sales conversations. I know the objections, the emotional triggers, the moment someone decides to say yes. That makes my copy better.
>
> Second, I'm full-stack. I can concept the angle, write the copy, and produce the creative - static, video, AI-generated. That means faster iteration and more testing volume without scaling the team.
>
> Third, I think in numbers. My finance background means I naturally think about ROAS, CPA, and testing velocity. I don't just make things that look good - I make things that perform."

### "What's your biggest failure?" (60-90 sec)

> "In my last sales role, I took on a territory that was a poor fit for my strengths. Instead of admitting that early and adapting, I kept pushing the same approach. I learned two things from that: first, self-awareness about what kind of work fits you is a strength, not a weakness. Second, when something isn't working, change the approach fast - don't just work harder at the wrong thing. Those lessons are actually core to creative strategy. You test, you read the data, and if a concept isn't working, you kill it and try a new angle. You don't throw more budget at a losing ad."

## Common Objections and Responses

### "You don't have creative/agency experience"
> "I don't have the title yet, but I have the underlying skills. Direct response copywriting is essentially what I've been doing in sales - writing persuasive messaging that drives action. The difference is now I can execute the full creative production too. And I bring something most creative candidates don't: real buyer psychology from thousands of sales conversations."

### "Your portfolio is all spec work"
> "That's fair - and it's exactly why I'm looking for this opportunity: to build a real track record. But I'd push back slightly on 'just spec.' Every piece in my portfolio was built with strategic intent - a specific audience, a specific angle, a hypothesis about what would work. I can walk you through the thinking behind any of them. And I can produce at volume, which means I'll generate real performance data fast."

### "Can you handle the pace of an agency?"
> "Absolutely. B2B SaaS sales is pure velocity - 300+ touchpoints a week, constant rejection, daily metric accountability. I'm used to operating at speed and being measured on output. The pace of creative production is different in kind, but the discipline transfers directly."

### "What if you decide you want to go back to sales?"
> "I've spent the last 6 months building deliberately toward creative strategy. This isn't a whim - it's where my skills and interests converge. The question I ask myself is: where does my work have the most impact? For me, that's creating the messaging that moves people, not delivering it one conversation at a time."

### "Why not just do marketing at a SaaS company?"
> "I specifically want direct response and performance creative because it's measurable. I come from a world where everything is tracked - pipeline, conversion rates, revenue. I want that same accountability applied to creative work. Brand marketing is valuable, but I want to see whether my ad converts within 48 hours, not wait 6 months for a brand lift study."

## Interview Frameworks Reference

### SOAR (preferred over STAR for creative roles)
- **S**ituation: Set the scene briefly
- **O**bjective: What was your creative vision or strategic goal? (This is better than "Task" because it shows intent)
- **A**ction: What specifically did you do?
- **R**esult: What happened? Use numbers when possible.

### CARL (good for showing growth/learning)
- **C**ontext: Brief background
- **A**ction: What you did
- **R**esult: What happened
- **L**earning: What you took away (shows adaptability - great for career changers)

### Pull-Bridge-Proof (for "why this career change?" questions)
- **Pull**: Why creative strategy excites you (lead with this, always)
- **Bridge**: How your background connects (sales psychology, analytical mind)
- **Proof**: What you've already built (portfolio, skills, knowledge)
