# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

This file accumulates your best interview stories over time. Each evaluation (Block F) adds new stories here. Instead of memorizing 100 answers, maintain 5-10 deep stories that you can bend to answer almost any behavioral question.

## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops offer` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

### [Creative Testing] The Angle That Landed
**Source:** Report #002 — Linear Agency — Creative Strategist (UGC for DTC eCommerce)
**S (Situation):** At Pax8, initial outreach to a key IT decision-maker segment was getting low reply rates.
**O (Objective):** Find the messaging angle that resonates with buyers who ignore vendor emails.
**A (Action):** Tested 5 different hook angles over 3 weeks. Analyzed which subject lines and opening lines drove replies. Iterated copy based on response patterns.
**R (Result):** Found the winning angle (pain-point led vs. product-led). Reply rate improved significantly, contributing to 125% quota attainment.
**Reflection:** This is exactly what creative testing is: hypothesis, test, read data, iterate. The medium was email, but the skill is the same as testing ad hooks.
**Best for questions about:** Creative testing methodology, data-driven iteration, performance optimization, adaptability

### [DR Theory] Deep Dive Into Direct Response Copywriting Canon
**Source:** Report #003 - Atomic HR - Creative Strategist (Paid Ads)

**O (Objective):** Master the direct response copywriting tradition and apply its frameworks to modern ad creative.

### [Hook Analysis] Component-Level Ad Breakdown (Hook/Body/Close)
**Source:** Report #009 -- Pearl West -- Performance Marketing Creative Strategist
**S (Situation):** During transition, analyzing competitor ads on Meta and TikTok to build creative analysis skills.
**O (Objective):** Develop a reusable method for diagnosing why ads succeed or fail at each component level, not just overall.
**A (Action):** Mapped Schwartz awareness levels to hook design (pattern interrupt + relevance), Georgi's RMBC to body structure (proof chain), and DR close principles to CTA + urgency. Applied this three-part lens to audit 50+ competitor ads across health/wellness and DTC verticals. Documented patterns: which hook types work at each awareness level, which proof structures hold attention, which close formats drive action.
**R (Result):** Built a reusable creative analysis framework. Created spec campaigns with 3+ hook variations per awareness level. Can diagnose a weak ad's specific failure point (bad hook vs. weak proof vs. missing urgency).
**Reflection:** Most creatives A/B test whole ads. Breaking into hook/body/close means you only change the weak part. Faster iteration, clearer signals, higher hit rate on new concepts.
**Best for questions about:** Creative analysis methodology, hook rate optimization, testing roadmaps, diagnosing underperforming ads, structured creative thinking
