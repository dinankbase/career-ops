# Mode: contact -- Multi-Channel Outreach

Generate targeted outreach across multiple channels. Adapted from LinkedIn-only to support email drip sequences, Twitter/X DMs, and referral outreach alongside LinkedIn.

**Input:** Company name + role (required), channel preference (optional: `linkedin`, `email`, `twitter`, `referral`, `all`)

Default: generate all applicable channels.

---

## Step 1: Identify Targets

Use WebSearch and LinkedIn to find:

1. **Hiring manager** for the team (Creative Director, Head of Growth, CMO, VP Marketing)
2. **Assigned recruiter** (if visible on the job listing)
3. **2-3 team peers** (people in a similar role at the company)
4. **Founder/CEO** (if small company <50 people)

Select **primary target**: the person who would benefit most from the candidate being there. Usually the hiring manager.

Find their:
- LinkedIn profile URL
- Email (if publicly available - check company website, Twitter bio, Hunter.io)
- Twitter/X handle (if active in the DR/DTC/marketing community)

---

## Step 2: Research the Target

Before drafting any message:
- Read their LinkedIn profile (recent posts, career history, shared interests)
- Check their Twitter for recent takes, content they engage with
- Look for something specific and genuine to reference (NOT generic flattery)
- Find one original insight about the company (CEO podcast, news, product angle)

---

## Step 3: Channel-Specific Messages

### A. LinkedIn Connection Request

**Framework: 3-sentence structure**

1. **Hook:** Specific observation about their company, their creative, or their content
2. **Proof:** Your biggest relevant capability, briefly
3. **Proposal:** Quick chat, no pressure

**Rules:**
- Maximum 300 characters (LinkedIn connection request limit)
- NO corporate-speak, NO "I'm passionate about"
- Something that makes them want to respond
- NEVER share phone number

**Creative Strategy version:**
```
Hi [Name], noticed [Company] is scaling [specific channel/product]. I put together a creative audit of your current Meta ads with some spec concepts. Would love to share it and chat for 15 min?
```

**Shorter version (if 300 char limit is tight):**
```
Hi [Name], I built some spec ad concepts for [Company] after studying your Meta Ad Library. Happy to share. 15 min chat?
```

**Sales fallback version:**
```
Hi [Name], [specific company insight]. I built [quantifiable achievement relevant to role]. Would love to learn about [specific topic] over a quick chat.
```

**After connection is accepted, follow-up message (no char limit):**
Include link to portfolio or spec work. Reference something specific from their profile.

---

### B. Email Outreach -- Spec Work Drip

Adapted from BowTiedCocoon's Ultimate Sequence. Instead of territory plan + lead list (sales), deliver creative audit + spec work (creative strategy).

**Core principle: Demonstrate, don't explicate.** Show you can do the job before being hired.

#### Email 1: The Opener
**Subject:** Thoughts on [Company] creative

```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] is [specific observation - running heavy Meta ads, scaling into TikTok, launching a new product line].

I've been studying your current ads and put together a brief creative audit with a few concepts I think would be worth testing. No cost, no strings - I built it because I genuinely find your brand interesting.

I'm a creative strategist with a direct response background. I can handle the full stack: copywriting, static, video, and AI-generated creative.

Would you be open to a quick look? I'll send the audit in my next email.

Best,
Daniel
daniel.nguyen.qc@gmail.com
```

#### Email 2: The Creative Audit (2 days later, reply to thread)
```
Hi [First Name],

As promised, here's the creative audit I put together for [Company].

[Attach or link Creative Audit - generated from interview-prep/creative-audit-template.md]

Key observations:
- [1-2 sentence summary of biggest gap]
- [1-2 sentence summary of your top spec concept]

Happy to walk through the thinking behind it.

Best,
Daniel
```

#### Email 3: The Spec Creative (2 days later, reply to thread)
```
Hi [First Name],

One more thing - I went ahead and produced [2-3 spec ad concepts] based on the audit.

[Link to spec work - portfolio site, Google Drive, or attached images]

The angle I'd test first: [brief description]. Here's why: [1 sentence rationale tied to customer insight].

If any of this resonates, I'd love to chat for 15 minutes about how I could contribute to [Company]'s creative.

Best,
Daniel
```

#### Email 4: Soft Close (3 days later, reply to thread)
```
Hi [First Name],

At this point I've shared a creative audit, spec concepts, and the strategic thinking behind them - all without a single minute of company training.

If you think there might be a fit, what would you suggest as a next step?

Best,
Daniel
[Phone Number]
```

#### Email 5: Final follow-up (5 days later, only if no reply)
Keep it very short:
```
[First Name]?
```

**Timing rules:**
- Email 1: Send anytime
- Email 2: 2 days after Email 1
- Email 3: 2 days after Email 2
- Email 4: 3 days after Email 3
- Email 5: 5 days after Email 4
- After Email 5 with no reply: move on. Do not send more.

### C. Twitter/X DM

**For people active in DR/DTC/marketing Twitter.**

**Value-first approach (NOT cold pitch):**

1. **Engage first (1-2 weeks):** Reply thoughtfully to 3-5 of their posts. Add genuine insight, not "great post!"
2. **Then DM:**

```
Hey [Name], been following your content on [topic]. Really liked your take on [specific post].

I've been building in performance creative - copywriting, video, AI-generated ads. Put together a spec campaign for [Brand/Company] after studying their ads.

Would love your thoughts if you have 5 min: [link to spec work]

No ask beyond feedback. Appreciate your time.
```

**Rules:**
- Never DM someone you haven't engaged with publicly first
- Keep it under 280 characters if possible
- Frame as seeking feedback, not seeking a job
- Include a link to specific work, not a generic portfolio
- If they respond positively, THEN mention you're looking for opportunities

**Ad breakdown thread strategy (builds credibility before DMs):**
Post public ad breakdowns tagging the brand. Format:
```
I broke down [Brand]'s top-performing Meta ads.

Here's what they're doing right, what's missing, and what I'd test instead:

[Thread with screenshots, analysis, spec concepts]
```

This attracts inbound from similar brands and builds credibility for future DMs.

### D. Referral / Warm Intro

**For connections who know someone at the target company.**

**Template for asking for intro:**
```
Hey [Connection Name],

I saw that you're connected with [Target Name] at [Company]. I'm making a move into performance creative strategy and they have a role that's a strong fit.

I put together a creative audit and some spec ad concepts for [Company]. Would you be comfortable introducing us? I'd keep it low-key - just sharing the work and seeing if there's a fit.

No worries at all if it's not the right ask. Appreciate you either way.

Daniel
```

**For Vietnamese professionals in advertising (network building):**
```
Hey [Name],

Fellow Viet here, based in Montreal. I'm building a career in direct response creative strategy - copywriting, video ads, AI creative production.

Saw your work at [Company/Role] and would love to learn from your experience in the space. Would you be open to a 15-min chat?

No agenda beyond connecting and learning.

Daniel
```

**Rules:**
- Only ask for intros from people you actually know
- Always offer to make it easy: "I can draft a quick blurb for you to forward"
- Never pressure
- Follow up once if no response, then drop it

## Step 5: Tracking

After outreach is sent, candidate should update `data/applications.md`:
- Status: change from `Evaluated` to `Applied` (or add new entry if no evaluation exists)
- Notes: which channel(s) used, date sent

**Message rules (apply to ALL channels):**
- NO corporate-speak
- NO "I'm passionate about..."
- NO generic flattery
- Something that makes them want to respond
- NEVER share phone number in LinkedIn messages (only in email after trust is established)
- ALWAYS include specific work (audit, spec creative, portfolio link)
- Frame as "sharing work" not "applying for a job"
